Marvel Spider-Man Games

Marvel Spider-Man

Marvel's Spider-Man is an action-adventure game that follows Peter Parker, a 23-year-old college graduate and research assistant who gained superhuman abilities after being bitten by a radioactive spider. The game is set in a fictionalized version of modern-day New York City and allows players to control Spider-Man throughout Manhattan. The gameplay involves swinging through the city, climbing buildings, and using web gadgets to fight enemies. The game features an open-world sandbox, where players can engage in combat, puzzles, and stealth sections. The narrative is driven by the threat of a new villain, Mister Negative, who poses a danger to the city and Peter's personal life. The game is praised for its authentic representation of Spider-Man, its engaging gameplay, and its faithful adaptation of the Marvel Comics universe.

Marvel Spider-Man Miles Morales

Marvel's Spider-Man: Miles Morales is an action-adventure game that centers around Miles Morales. A teenager who is adjusting to his new life in Harlem while following in the footsteps of his mentor, Peter Parker. When a power struggle between the Roxxon Energy Corporation and a high-tech criminal group called the Underground threatens his home, Miles must step up to protect his community and fully embrace his identity as Spider-Man. The game explores themes of responsibility, belonging, and the challenges of being a hero.

Marvel Spider-Man 2

Marvel's Spider-Man 2 is an action-adventure game that follows the story of Peter Parker and Miles Morales as they face new threats in Marvel's New York. The game features two main playable characters, each with unique abilities and storylines. Players can switch between Peter and Miles, each with their own combat abilities and skill trees. The game introduces new traversal mechanics, such as Web Wings, and enhances combat with new gadgets and parrying. The narrative is driven by the bond between Peter and the Venom symbiote , which alters his behavior and grants him powerful abilities. The game also includes side missions, collectibles, and the ability to switch between Peter and Miles for dedicated objectives. Marvel's Spider-Man 2 is available on PC and PlayStation 5, with a Microsoft Windows port released on January 30, 2025.

Marvel Spider-Man DLC

Marvel's Spider-Man: The City That Never Sleeps is a DLC that expands the narrative of the game by three months after its conclusion. It follows Spider-Man as he faces a new crime wave led by the mob boss Hammerhead. The DLC is presented in three chapters: The Heist, Turf Wars, and Silver Lining, each offering new story content and missions. Players can unlock new gadgets and suits by progressing through the main story or completing tasks. The DLC is accessible from the main menu and takes place in a separate instance of New York City, excluding the main game's story, side missions, and random crimes.

Marvel Spider-Man Suits

Suits are collectible and wearable items that grant active power-ups, called suit powers, in Marvel's Spider-Man. Suits first have to be unlocked, either by completing a specific mission, or reaching a certain level, and then purchased with tokens. While each suit comes with a signature power of its own, these can be swapped depending on players' gameplay style or preference. Players may also customize their suits with up to three suit mods, which grant various passive bonuses.

Marvel Spider-Man Miles Morales Suits

There are 19 suits available for Miles Morales, each offering different aesthetics and gameplay enhancements. Suits can be unlocked by completing specific missions, reaching certain levels, or collecting resources like Activity Tokens and Tech Parts. Each suit often comes with a suit mod, which provides passive bonuses that can enhance gameplay. For example, the Into the Spider-Verse Suit has a mod that allows it to mimic the aesthetic of different Spider-Verse characters.

Marvel Spider-Man 2 Suits

There are 90 suits in the game: 68 in the base game, ten in the Digital Deluxe Edition, two purchasable in the Fly n' Fresh Suit Pack, and ten added through free updates. All suits also have three palette swaps known as Suit Styles, except for the Spider-Men's various live action and animated series suits, the Digital Deluxe Edition suits, the Fly and Fresh Suits, and the Hellfire Gala suits. For suits from the Spider-Verse animated films, the Screen Effect options Film Style Animation and Comic Book Effects go into effect.
